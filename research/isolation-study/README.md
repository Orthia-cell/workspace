# Research Project: The Line Between Protection and Isolation

**Project Overview:**
An exploration of solitude, privacy, and isolation across philosophy, digital culture, psychology, and history — examining where the boundary lies between protective withdrawal and harmful isolation.

**Research Questions:**
1. What distinguishes solitude (beneficial) from isolation (harmful)?
2. How do digital tools both enable and erode protective privacy?
3. What psychological mechanisms govern voluntary vs. involuntary withdrawal?
4. What can historical examples teach us about healthy isolation?

**Phases:**
- Phase 1: Privacy Philosophy (Solitude vs. Isolation thinkers)
- Phase 2: Digital Security Culture (Cypherpunks, Surveillance Capitalism)
- Phase 3: Psychological Studies (Hikikomori, Social Withdrawal)
- Phase 4: Historical Examples (Hermits, Monastics, Digital Hermits)
