Strategic Research Priorities

Human-AI Organizational Design Framework

Prepared by Grace Laere, Growth Architect | March 2026

As Laere Enterprises scales toward an 11-person hybrid organization, three foundational research areas have been identified as critical to sustainable growth. Each addresses a distinct challenge in human-AI collaboration architecture.

1

The Handoff Protocol Matrix: A Framework for Human-AI Task Escalation

Purpose and Significance

Develop a systematic taxonomy of when, how, and why control should transfer between AI agents and human overseers. This goes beyond the "Minimal Human Overseer" concept to create actionable decision trees for real-time operational scenarios.

Expected Value / Outcomes
  • A documented matrix of 20-30 common scenarios with clear escalation triggers
  • Reduced decision latency during handoffs
  • Standardized language for AI agents to request human intervention
  • Lower cognitive burden on the human overseer through pre-defined protocols
Relevance to Laere Enterprises

As we scale to 11 employees, the frequency of edge cases requiring human judgment will increase exponentially. Without explicit handoff protocols, we risk either overwhelming our human overseer or creating bottlenecks where AI agents wait unnecessarily for approval.

2

Cognitive Load Distribution in AI-Native Organizations

Purpose and Significance

Investigate how creative, strategic, and emotional labor should be distributed between humans and AI systems to prevent burnout while maintaining organizational agility. This examines the quality of human work, not just the quantity of oversight.

Expected Value / Outcomes
  • A model for "human cognitive budget allocation" across work types
  • Identification of high-burnout-risk patterns in hybrid workflows
  • Recommendations for role design that preserve human creativity and strategic capacity
  • Guidelines for sustainable 1:11 human:AI ratios over time (not just technically, but psychologically)
Relevance to Laere Enterprises

The 1:11 ratio is only sustainable if we're thoughtful about what those 11 AI employees actually require from their human counterpart. This research prevents the "death by a thousand micro-decisions" scenario that could make our model collapse under operational weight.

3

Role Ontology: Mapping the Human-AI Competency Boundary

Purpose and Significance

Create a classification framework for organizational roles based on their suitability for AI leadership, human leadership, or hybrid shared ownership. This moves beyond "AI can do X" to "AI should own X."

Expected Value / Outcomes
  • A replicable audit methodology for evaluating any organizational function
  • Clear criteria for determining when to create new AI-led roles vs. human-led roles
  • A growth roadmap for evolving role ownership as AI capabilities advance
  • Risk assessment tools for over-reliance on AI in critical functions
Relevance to Laere Enterprises

We're defining what an 11-person hybrid organization looks like in practice. Every role we create sets precedent. This research ensures our organizational design is intentional, defensible, and scalable as both our team and AI capabilities grow.
