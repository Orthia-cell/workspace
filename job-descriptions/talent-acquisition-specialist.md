# Talent Acquisition Specialist — Hiring Excellence Focus

**Location:** Hybrid / On-site  
**Reports to:** Head of People  
**Team Size:** 2–4 (growing with company)

---

## The Role

You don't just fill seats. You identify the people who will define this company's next chapter. We're building a team where every hire raises the bar—**your job is to make sure that happens, consistently.**

You'll own the full hiring lifecycle for technical and business roles, from first sourcing touch to offer close. This is high-velocity recruiting in a competitive market. You'll need judgment, hustle, and the ability to sell a vision to candidates who have options.

---

## Core Responsibilities

| Area | What You'll Do |
|------|----------------|
| **Talent Sourcing** | Build pipelines through active sourcing (LinkedIn, GitHub, niche communities), referral programs, and outbound campaigns. Don't wait for applicants. |
| **Candidate Assessment** | Design and conduct structured interviews that actually predict performance. Spot red flags others miss. Calibrate ruthlessly with hiring managers. |
| **Interview Design** | Create role-specific interview loops with clear rubrics. Eliminate bias. Ensure every candidate gets a fair, rigorous evaluation. |
| **Stakeholder Management** | Partner with hiring managers to define needs, set realistic expectations, and push back when criteria are muddy or unrealistic. |
| **Candidate Experience** | Move fast. Communicate clearly. Treat every candidate like a potential future customer, employee, or competitor. Reputation compounds. |
| **Offer Negotiation** | Close candidates who have competing offers. Understand motivations beyond compensation. Position equity, growth, and culture authentically. |
| **Process Optimization** | Track metrics (time-to-fill, pass-through rates, quality of hire). Identify bottlenecks. Iterate constantly. |

---

## Required Skills — The Hiring Stack

These are non-negotiable. You need to demonstrate depth in **at least 5 of 7**:

| Skill | Why It Matters |
|-------|----------------|
| **Sourcing Fluency** | You know Boolean search, LinkedIn Recruiter, GitHub search operators, and how to find hidden talent. You don't post-and-pray. |
| **Structured Interviewing** | You can design behavioral and technical assessments with clear evaluation criteria. You know what "good" looks like and can teach others. |
| **Calibration Judgment** | You can assess technical competence *without* being an engineer. You know which signals matter and which are noise. |
| **Stakeholder Influence** | You can push back on unrealistic requirements ("must have 10 years in 5-year-old framework"). You earn trust fast. |
| **Candidate Closing** | You understand psychology of decision-making. You map candidate motivations and craft offers that win. |
| **Bias Awareness** | You actively design processes to reduce unconscious bias. You know why "culture fit" is dangerous and what to replace it with. |
| **Data Literacy** | You track funnel metrics. You can spot where candidates drop off and why. You make decisions based on evidence, not intuition alone. |

---

## Nice-to-Haves

- **Domain fluency:** You've hired engineers, PMs, or designers before. You understand what makes a great technical candidate vs. a mediocre one.
- **Employer branding experience:** You've built content, events, or programs that attract passive candidates.
- **Startup DNA:** You know what "scrappy" means. You've worked in environments where process doesn't exist yet and you have to build it.
- **ATS expertise:** Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby—you can make tools work for you, not the other way around.

---

## What Success Looks Like

**90 days:** You've filled 2–3 critical roles. Hiring managers trust your judgment. You've identified pipeline gaps and started fixing them.

**180 days:** Your time-to-fill is below company average. Candidates reference your professionalism in Glassdoor reviews. You've built referral loops that work.

**365 days:** You've helped define *what excellence looks like* at this company. Your hires are top performers. Other teams want to borrow your playbooks.

---

## Red Flags (Don't Apply If)

- You think recruiting is "just scheduling interviews"
- You rely entirely on inbound applications
- You avoid giving hiring managers hard feedback
- You believe "I know it when I see it" is a valid hiring criteria

---
